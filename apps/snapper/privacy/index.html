Privacy Policy

Snapper — the skunkware fishing app.

Last updated: June 21, 2026

The short version: everything stays on your phone. Snapper has no accounts and no servers. skunkware never receives your catches, your saved spots, your photos, or your location. There is no tracking, no analytics, and no ads.

What Snapper stores

Everything you create in Snapper is saved only in the app's private storage on your device:

  • Catches — the photo, species, length/weight, notes, time, and (if available) the GPS location of each fish you log.
  • Saved spots — the coordinates, an optional name, and an optional photo for fishing spots you save.

None of this is transmitted to skunkware or anyone else. It lives in app-private storage, is excluded from Android cloud backups, and is permanently deleted when you remove a catch or spot in the app, or when you uninstall Snapper.

Permissions

  • Camera — to photograph your catches and spots. Photos are saved on your device only.
  • Location — to stamp a catch with where you caught it, to save a spot, to center the map on you, and to find water near you. Your location is used on the device and stored on the device; it is not sent to skunkware.
  • Internet — to load map imagery, search for places, and fetch reference photos for fish species (see below).

Fish identification

Snapper identifies fish entirely on your device using a built-in model. Your photos are never uploaded for identification — or for any other reason.

Third-party services

To show maps and reference material, the app talks directly to a few public services. It sends them only what's needed for the feature you're using — never your saved catches, spots, or personal photos:

  • OpenStreetMap / Overpass and the map-tile providers CARTO and Esri — receive the map area you are currently viewing, to draw the map and find nearby water.
  • Your device's location service (typically Google, via Android's geocoder) — when you search for a place or the app converts coordinates to a place name.
  • Wikipedia — to fetch a reference photo for a fish species you view.

These providers handle that data under their own privacy policies. Snapper does not share your identity with them — it has no identity for you to share.

Children

Snapper is not directed at children and collects no personal information for advertising or tracking from anyone.
