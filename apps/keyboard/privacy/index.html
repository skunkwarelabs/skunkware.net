Privacy Policy

key🅱️oard — the skunkware keyboard.

Last updated: June 27, 2026

The short version: nothing you type leaves your phone. key🅱️oard has no accounts, no servers, and no internet access at all. skunkware never receives what you type, and there is no tracking, no analytics, and no ads.

What key🅱️oard collects

Nothing. key🅱️oard is a keyboard (an Android input method). Like any keyboard, the system shows it the text field you are editing so it can insert text — but key🅱️oard does not record, save, or transmit what you type. It keeps no history, no learned profile, and no log of your keystrokes. Text is read only momentarily, in memory, to do its job (for example, to delete the character before the cursor), and is never written anywhere.

No network access

key🅱️oard requests no internet permission and makes no network calls. It cannot send your typing — or anything else — off your device, because it has no way to reach the network in the first place. There are no API keys and no third-party services.

On-device features

  • Glide (swipe) typing is decoded entirely on your device, by comparing your gesture against a word list that is bundled inside the app. No dictionary lookups or predictions are fetched from anywhere.
  • Theming reads your system's Material You colors locally to style the keys. That information stays on the device.

Permissions

  • Vibrate — the only permission key🅱️oard requests. It is used solely to produce the small haptic tap when you press a key. That's it.

Children

key🅱️oard is not directed at children and collects no personal information for advertising or tracking from anyone.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date above.
